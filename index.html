Narcolepsy

A short introduction to narcolepsy

In 2013 I was diagnosed with narcolepsy. Narcolepsy is a chronic sleep disorder that effects the control of sleep and wakefulness. The disorder is characterized by excessive sleepiness, sleep attacks, sleep paralysis, hallucinations and in most cases cataplexy(a sudden loss of muscle control).

The disorder is caused by a autoimmune disease that causes a deficiency in hypocretin, a chemical that is necessary for the brain to stay awake. As a neurotransmitter, hypocretin controls when we are awake or asleep by acting on different groups cell or neurons.
